## Why anon.li?

A lot of privacy tools fail in one of two directions: they're too fiddly to use every day, or they put "private" on the label while still keeping everything in plaintext on a server. We tried to avoid both. Drop encrypts in your browser before anything uploads, so the keys never reach us. Alias forwards mail in real time instead of warehousing it. And the application code is [open source](https://github.com/anondotli/anon.li), so none of that has to be taken on faith.

## What We Offer

### Alias - Private Email Forwarding

Your email address is your digital identity. It's used to track you across the web, target you with ads, and link your accounts together.

With anon.li Alias, you can:
- Create email aliases that forward to your real inbox
- Reply from aliases without revealing your real address
- Block spam with one click by disabling an alias
- Add PGP encryption for end-to-end security
- Use custom domains for professional branding

### Drop - Encrypted File Sharing

Sharing sensitive files shouldn't mean trusting a third party. With anon.li Drop, your files are encrypted in your browser before they ever leave your device.

Features include:
- AES-256-GCM encryption with keys you control
- Automatic expiration and download limits
- Optional password protection
- Zero-knowledge storage - we literally cannot see your files
- Free account uploads with public link-only downloads

## How we keep ourselves honest

The platform code is [open source](https://github.com/anondotli/anon.li), so you can check the claims above against what actually runs instead of trusting a marketing page. We collect only what the service needs to work — no tracking pixels of our own, no analytics cookies, nothing sold on. And the business model is subscriptions, not ads, which is the only reason the incentives line up: we make money when you pay us, not when we learn more about you. The free tier stays free because a privacy tool that's paywalled out of reach isn't much of a privacy tool.
